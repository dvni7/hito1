## What this is

Hito 2 extends the Hito 1 baseline in two ways:

1. **Dual-target modeling:** trains a second model on `is_top3` (podium or not) alongside the locked `is_top10` model. The two targets use identical feature sets to enable side-by-side what-if comparisons.
2. **Error analysis:** structured Brier-score analysis sliced by strategy type, circuit type, and weather — identifying where and why the model fails, with concrete failure-mode hypotheses.

The central finding: when `is_top10` is near-certain under both strategies (P > 0.85 for both A and B), it loses discrimination and gives no decision signal. `is_top3` remains sensitive in exactly those cases. The Qatar 2024 scenario shows this: is_top10 Δ = +0.002, is_top3 Δ = −0.286.

## Key findings

**Error analysis:** the worst single prediction cell is one_stop × wet races (Brier = 0.264, 2.17× the overall mean). Root cause: no safety-car or rain-onset signal in the dataset. Full analysis in `error_analysis.md`.

**What-if finding:** Qatar 2024, P2 start — is_top10 Δ = +0.002 (no signal), is_top3 Δ = −0.286 (decisive). The two targets disagree in 17.5% of 1-stop vs 2-stop comparisons in the test set. Full analysis in `whatif_comparison.md`.

**Confounding limitation:** strategy choice is not independent of car pace, weather, or race incidents. Model outputs are associative estimates, not causal predictions. Documented in `leakage_audit.md`.
